Subtheme: Trends and Patterns in Drug Overdose Fatalities in Allegheny County

Introduction

Accidental overdose deaths have surged in Allegheny County, Pennsylvania, highlighting a critical public health concern that this analysis seeks to explore. This exploratory data analysis (EDA) aims to identify trends in these incidents by examining demographic factors such as age, gender, race, and the drugs involved, as well as geographic details. Using data published by the Western Pennsylvania Regional Data Center, this analysis seeks to uncover key insights to better understand the crisis and help develop strategies for prevention.

About the dataset

The dataset contains 7,136 records of fatal accidental overdose incidents in Allegheny County, collected by the county's Medical Examiner’s Office and published by the Western Pennsylvania Regional Data Center. It includes details such as the date and time of death, the decedent’s age, sex, race, drugs involved in the overdose (up to 10 substances), and the zip codes for both the incident location and the decedent’s residence (although this data is no longer available). The dataset was created on February 14, 2019, last updated on September 11, 2024, and is available in CSV format. It spans multiple years and provides insights into the demographic and geographic patterns of overdose deaths, aiding in the analysis of this ongoing health crisis.
